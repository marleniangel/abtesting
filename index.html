Part 1: Identifying Usability Problems

Picking a Webpage

Jala Studio Original Website

I chose this website because I noticed numerous glaring accessibility issues with the homepage like responsiveness and low contrast.

Finding Problems

1. Users need to understand how to toggle on dropdown menus in order to access the schedule for yoga events. This may not be intuitive especially because of the very low contrast of the dropdown menu bar.
2. The dropdown menu bar is also not apparent to be clickable, affecting the learnability of the site.
3. The traits of the dropdown menus are different in that not all lead to a different site while a couple do. However, all minus the “home” have a dropdown. This affects both the memorability and efficiency of the site.
4. To be able to purchase artwork or a gift certificate, the issue persists of not being able to distinguish whether text is clickable or not. Users have to hover over all text to figure out which words link to relevant parts of the site which affects the learnability of the site.

Accessibility

As expected, WebAIM detected glaring contrast issues with the site. Most of the text on the home webpage has very low contrast against the white background site. Additionally, the tool detected a missing heading and empty and redundant links. I also noticed the redundancy of one of the clickable elements but I was not aware of the missing headings. I agree with the low contrast errors because this was one of the main issues that caused me to choose this site to redesign.
